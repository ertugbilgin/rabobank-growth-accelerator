M3 · Resilience

The product survives the move-in, the move-out, the breakup.

House composition changes — roommates come and go. The hardest unsolved problem in shared subs is what happens when membership shifts. This is where automation becomes a relationship product, not just a payment tool.

The wedge · the recurring small amount

Who builds for the silent monthly split?

Tikkie sends one-off requests — you can't auto-tikkie €4.50 to three people every month. Bunq lets you split a sub-account but doesn't detect subscriptions. Splitwise tracks but doesn't move money. The 'tiny recurring shared expense' is everywhere — and nowhere as a product. The job ad literally lists "subscriptions" as a target use case. This is the most direct answer to that line.
